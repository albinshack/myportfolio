Project Overview

This project explores customer churn patterns using data-driven analysis and visualizations. By examining factors such as contract type, monthly charges, tenure, and service usage, the analysis identifies key drivers of customer attrition. The goal is to provide actionable insights and recommendations to reduce churn and improve customer retention.

Key findings include:

  • High churn observed among month-to-month contract customers.
  • New customers and high-paying users are more likely to leave.
  • Data visualizations such as bar charts, histograms, and box plots are used to illustrate trends.

Recommendations from the analysis focus on enhancing onboarding, offering loyalty incentives, and promoting long-term contracts to maximize customer lifetime value.

Key Insights

  • High Churn Rate: Approximately 88.3% of customers have churned
  • Contract Type Impact: Month-to-month contracts show the highest churn (nearly 100%)
  • Pricing Sensitivity: Customers with higher monthly charges are more likely to churn
  • Customer Tenure: New customers are more likely to leave

Recommendations

  • Offer discounts and incentives for long-term contracts
  • Improve onboarding experience for new customers
  • Provide loyalty rewards for high-value customers
  • Introduce retention strategies for month-to-month users
  • Reevaluate pricing to align with customer expectations
